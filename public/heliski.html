01 Project Overview

ONE PLATFORM,
EVERY HELISKI
TRIP ON EARTH.

Heliski International is a platform designed to serve as a global hub for heliskiing adventure seekers and experience providers. The project encompassed the design and development of a modern website and booking marketplace that enables users to discover, compare, and book curated heliski trips in premier mountain destinations worldwide.

The system was designed to simplify global heliski trip discovery and booking for adventure enthusiasts, while giving operators a powerful digital channel to reach customers and manage their listings.

Website Booking Marketplace Tour Operator
  The Problem

A SITE WITH
NO ENGINE
BEHIND IT.

Heliski International already had basic user-facing designs, but lacked many essential features for a complete travel platform. The absence of an admin portal prevented efficient management of operators, experiences, destinations, customer details, and reviews.

Without dedicated pages for personal profiles and operator information, travelers found it difficult to manage bookings or interact with operators. Additionally, the existing site was not fully mobile responsive, resulting in a poor user experience.

The real gap: A pretty front-end with no operational backbone no way to manage the marketplace at scale, and no way for users to trust or track what they were booking.

1.0 Design Strategy

SOLVING FOR BOTH
SIDES OF THE TABLE

A marketplace only works if it serves both sides travelers searching for the experience of a lifetime, and operators trying to fill seats on expensive, weather-dependent trips. Every design decision had to hold up for both.

USER NEEDS
Easy comparisonfilter and compare trips by destination, budget, and operator without leaving the search results.
Trust before bookingverified reviews, operator credentials, and transparent safety policies visible upfront.
A real profile, not just a transactionsaved favourites, booking history, and direct messaging with operators.
Mobile-first accessresearch and book a high-value adventure trip from a phone, not just a desktop.
BUSINESS NEEDS
Operational control at scalea centralized admin portal to manage operators, listings, customers, and reviews without manual spreadsheets.
Automated onboardingstreamlined operator onboarding workflows replacing what had previously been manual, email-based processes.
A real digital sales channeloperators gain direct access to a qualified, high-intent adventure travel audience.
Reduced support overheadbuilt-in messaging and transparent listings cut down on pre-booking inquiry volume.

2.1 Wireframe & Prototyping

EXPANDING ON
AN EXISTING
FOUNDATION.

BUILDING ON
WHAT EXISTED

For this project, the wireframing and prototyping process began with an already-established user-side design and UI design kit. Our team focused on developing and refining key new areas: detailed user and operator profiles, favourites, and an engaging blog section.

We also built out the complete admin portal designing every page and workflow covering experiences, destinations, operators, customers, settings, reviews, and operator requests. Each component was created with fully mobile-responsive layouts in mind, ensuring seamless usability across devices.

Design discipline: This approach ensured consistency with the existing visual language while expanding the platform's functionality achieving a cohesive, user-friendly interface rather than a bolt-on admin tool that felt disconnected from the consumer brand.

05 Reflection

WHAT I LEARNED

Designing the invisible half of a marketplace

The admin portal never gets seen by end users, but it's what makes the consumer experience possible at scale. I learned to treat internal tools with the same craft as customer-facing screens because a broken admin workflow eventually becomes a broken customer experience.

Cross-functional collaboration across 5 disciplines

Regular sprint planning and retrospective meetings provided structured opportunities to address challenges, clarify requirements, and refine design and functionality. Close coordination among developers, designers, product managers, QA, and business stakeholders kept goals aligned and workflows streamlined.

Extending an existing design system without breaking it

Working from an established UI kit meant every new screen profiles, admin, blog had to feel native to the existing brand. This taught me real discipline in extending systems rather than reinventing them.

Trust signals matter more in high-ticket travel

Heliski trips cost thousands of dollars and depend on operator safety records. I learned that for high-stakes purchases, UI decisions around credibility reviews, credentials, transparency carry as much weight as the booking flow itself.
