01 Project Overview

ONE APP FOR
NEPAL'S HARDEST
EXAM.

The Loksewa Aayog (Public Service Commission) exam is one of the most competitive examinations in Nepal , hundreds of thousands of aspirants sit it every year for a limited number of government positions. The stakes are high. The preparation is gruelling.

Loksewa Pro was designed to be the definitive study companion , consolidating quizzes, practice sets, exercise sets, audio books, course materials, and current affairs into a single, beautifully designed mobile app.

Mobile App EdTech iOS & Android Consumer
  The Problem

EVERY EXISTING
APP WAS
FAILING USERS.

Existing Loksewa preparation apps shared the same failures: incomplete and outdated study materials, no structured progress tracking, poor personalization, and frustrating navigation. Premium features rarely justified their price.

Core insight: Aspirants weren't just failing the exam , they were failing because their preparation tools were unreliable, demotivating, and impossible to use consistently offline.

1.0 User Understanding

WHO WE DESIGNED
FOR

Through user surveys and review analysis, we identified two distinct user archetypes with different study behaviours but shared core needs around reliability, structure, and motivation.

👨‍🎓
Ramesh, 24
First-time Loksewa aspirant

A recent BSc graduate from outside the valley preparing for his first Loksewa attempt. Studies 3–4 hours daily using his phone, often on bus rides. Has patchy internet access.

Needs: Structured syllabus, offline access, clear progress tracking to stay motivated during a long preparation period.

👩‍💼
Sita, 28
Repeat aspirant, 2nd attempt

Works part-time while preparing. Failed once , knows her weak subjects. Needs targeted practice, not broad review. Checks the app during breaks and late evenings.

Needs: Subject-specific practice, ability to revisit mistakes, performance analytics to identify gaps fast.

🏙️
Bikash, 22
Urban aspirant, premium user

Lives in Kathmandu, has reliable internet. Competitive , wants to know how he ranks. Willing to pay for premium if the quality is genuinely better than free alternatives.

Needs: Daily quizzes, leaderboards, high-quality current affairs, audio books for multitasking.

👩‍🏫
Kamala, 31
Rural aspirant, limited connectivity

Teacher from Surkhet, intermittent internet. Needs materials downloaded in advance. Trusts the app if content is accurate and updated , one wrong answer and she loses faith.

Needs: Reliable offline access, verified content accuracy, simple interface that doesn't require learning.

1.1 Research & Discovery

WHAT THE DATA
TOLD US

We ran a three-pronged research process , analysing existing apps through user reviews, surveying exam aspirants directly, and studying competitor products to map gaps in the market.

⭐

App review analysis

Systematically analysed 1-star and 5-star reviews of competing Loksewa apps. Patterns: top praise for "organized content" and "offline mode." Top complaints: "glitches," "outdated questions," and "confusing navigation."

📋

Aspirant surveys

Surveys with exam aspirants surfaced insights on study habits, preferred learning methods (quiz-based over passive reading), and key challenges , inconsistent study schedules and inability to track weak subjects.

🔍

Competitive audit

Most competitors provide fragmented resources with limited personalization. None combined all formats (quiz, audio, practice sets, current affairs) in a single, well-designed experience with reliable offline access.

THE CORE DESIGN CHALLENGE

Ensuring the accuracy and completeness of study materials was critical , many existing apps contained errors or outdated content, directly misleading users during high-stakes preparation.

Technical challenges included app stability, server connectivity, and offline access , all of which had to be designed for from the ground up, not patched after the fact.

Key design principle adopted: Trust is the product. Every content decision, every loading state, every error message had to communicate reliability , because one wrong question costs a user's confidence in the entire app.

FOUR PROBLEMS.
FOUR SOLUTIONS.

1

Solution

2.1 Wireframe & Prototyping

MID-FIDELITY
FROM DAY ONE.

WHY WE SKIPPED LOW-FI

For this project, wireframing began directly at mid-fidelity , we had a clear project brief, well-defined requirements, a tight 2-month deadline, and an already-established user-side design and UI design kit.

This approach let us focus immediately on structure and user flow without getting bogged down in early-stage sketches. It accelerated the feedback loop with the project manager significantly.

My team focused on developing and refining: detailed user and operator profiles, favourites, a blog section, a complete admin portal covering experiences, destinations, operators, customers, settings, reviews, and operator requests , all with fully mobile-responsive layouts.

Design choice: I designed both dark and light interface variants to increase accessibility and appeal , giving users genuine choice rather than forcing a single aesthetic. This decision directly influenced the app's positive reviews on the Play Store.

2.2 Key Design Decisions

BEFORE &
AFTER

Before

Flat list of all study materials

Users were dropped into an overwhelming flat list of hundreds of questions with no sense of structure, priority, or progress.

→
After

Structured practice sets with clear progress states

Practice sets show Not Started / In Progress / Completed states, question counts, and time estimates upfront , so users always know where they are and what's next.

Before

No daily habit mechanism

Users had to decide what to study every day , choice fatigue led to inconsistent sessions and app abandonment.

→
After

Daily Quiz as a front-and-center anchor

The Daily Quiz feature sits at the top of the home screen , a low-friction daily entry point that builds a study habit without requiring the user to make a decision.

Before

Single colour theme , light only

One visual mode suited city users on new devices but frustrated rural users studying at night or on older, low-brightness screens.

→
After

Dark and light themes , user's choice

Both themes fully designed and implemented. Dark mode reduced eye strain for night studying , directly mentioned in positive Play Store reviews as a differentiator from competitors.

Before

No feedback on wrong answers

Users completed quizzes but had no way to understand why they got something wrong or revisit mistakes , the learning loop was broken.

→
After

Mistake review + subject performance reports

After every session, users can review wrong answers with explanations. Weekly subject-level performance reports surface weak areas automatically , turning data into a study plan.

05 Reflection

WHAT I LEARNED

Design for trust, not just usability

In EdTech , especially for high-stakes exams , one wrong answer can break a user's trust in the entire product. Every content decision needed to communicate accuracy. I learned that in some contexts, perceived reliability is the primary UX metric.

Mid-fidelity wireframing accelerated delivery

Skipping low-fidelity sketches and jumping to mid-fidelity , given the clear brief and existing design kit , cut the feedback loop in half. For small teams with tight deadlines, this is a principle I now apply deliberately.

Habit design is UX design

The Daily Quiz wasn't just a feature , it was a retention mechanism. I learned that for a study app, the most important design question isn't "Is this usable?" but "Will users come back tomorrow?" That changes every priority in the product.

Offline-first thinking changed how I approach mobile

Designing for users with unreliable internet forced me to think about every state , loading, offline, partial sync, error. This constraint produced a better product for all users, not just rural ones. Constraints are design tools.
