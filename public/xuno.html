1.0 Project Overview

CROSS-BORDER
PAYMENTS,
REIMAGINED.

Request to Pay (r2px) is a platform designed to revolutionize cross-border payments, empowering individuals and businesses in Nepal to seamlessly receive money from the USA. Unlike traditional remittance methods, r2px allows Nepalese users to send payment requests directly to US contacts through an easy-to-use webapp or mobile interface.

Integrated with trusted national channels such as IME Pay and connectIPS, the platform supports secure transactions, real-time payment tracking, and settlement directly into the recipient's bank account or digital wallet.

Web Application Mobile App Fintech Cross-Border
  The Problem

HIDDEN FEES.
SLOW TRANSFERS.
NO TRUST.

Other cross-border payment platforms often charge high fees baked into the transferred amount: hidden charges like currency conversion markups, bank processing costs, and service commissions. The process is often slow, with unclear exchange rates and limited transparency, causing frustration and uncertainty for recipients in Nepal.

The opportunity: R2px leverages Nepal's National Payment Infrastructure and real-time tracking, letting users send requests through familiar local platforms like IME Pay and connectIPS, with NRB-regulated exchange rates and flexible settlement speeds, from instant to standard.

2.0 My Role · Research Process

FLOWS FIRST.
THEN BENCHMARKS.

UI/UX DESIGN
PROCESS

I began the design process by mapping detailed user flows and creating flow diagrams to ensure a clear, logical journey for every major action. This step provided the team with a solid framework before moving into actual interface design.

To create a best-in-class user experience, I conducted extensive competitor research, examining platforms like Wise, Remitly, Western Union, MoneyGram, Instarem, and Panda Remit, but also focusing on Nepal-centric platforms such as eSewa Money Transfer, IME Pay, NEO Money Transfer, connectIPS, and Fonepay. This helped us identify user expectations, must-have features, and interface standards for seamless, transparent money transfers.

3.0 Design Phase · Pain Point Analysis

WHAT MAKES
REMITTANCE PAINFUL

1

Hidden Fees Baked Into Exchange Rates

Solution

Displayed the NRB-regulated bank's buying rate directly on the request screen, before a request is even sent.

2

Unfamiliar Settlement Channels

Solution

Integrated with IME Pay and connectIPS: settlement directly into bank accounts or wallets already familiar to Nepalese users.

3

No Visibility Into Transfer Status

Solution

Built real-time payment tracking as part of the request-based flow, leveraging Nepal's National Payment Infrastructure.

4

One-Size-Fits-All Transfer Speed

Solution

Designed flexible settlement speed options, from instant to standard, so users choose based on their own urgency.

6.0 Reflection

WHAT I LEARNED

Rate transparency is the product, not decoration

In remittance, showing the bank's buying rate clearly, before a request is even sent, isn't a small UI detail. It's the thing that differentiates a platform from competitors that bury fees in the exchange rate. That shaped how I prioritised every other screen in this project.

Fintech is a different kind of design challenge

As my first fintech project, I learned that designing for money transfers means every pixel carries weight: a misplaced button or unclear label doesn't just confuse users, it erodes trust in real transactions. This project taught me to balance regulatory constraints with smooth user experience, and why clarity matters more than visual flair in financial products.
